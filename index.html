About

Financial markets represent perhaps the most challenging frontier for machine learning: an environment characterized by extreme non-stationarity, low signal-to-noise ratios, and high-stakes adversarial dynamics. As the industry shifts toward agentic systems—autonomous entities capable of reasoning and planning—the gap between empirical success and theoretical understanding widens.

  • A unique laboratory for robustness: continual learning under constant distribution shift
  • The vanguard of AI safety & regulation: verification, certified robustness, governed autonomy
  • Agentic systems for mission-critical deployment in finance

XForecast Challenge

Overview

The XForecast Challenge is a community competition on news-grounded financial forecasting, organized as part of the ICML 2026 Workshop on Advances in Financial AI. The challenge explores how modern AI models can integrate financial time-series data with unstructured textual information to predict future market behavior.

Participants are given historical stock price data together with associated financial news articles sourced from Reuters. The task is to build models that leverage both modalities to predict the future closing price of stocks four weeks ahead. The dataset covers multiple years of market activity, enabling participants to explore approaches that combine numerical market signals with textual information.

  • Dataset: historical stock prices paired with Reuters financial news
  • Multimodal forecasting using time-series and textual signals
  • Task: predict the closing price of stocks four weeks ahead
  • Evaluation: prediction accuracy measured using a Hit Rate metric
  • Training period: 2019–2022 · Evaluation period: 2023

The competition focuses on the top 100 stocks by market capitalization and encourages participants to explore multimodal learning, representation learning, and forecasting methods for financial markets.
